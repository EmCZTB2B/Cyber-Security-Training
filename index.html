Section 2 — Password Security

Password Best Practice

80% of all data breaches involve stolen or weak passwords (Verizon DBIR). Your password is often the only barrier between an attacker and our client data.

✅ DO THIS
Use minimum 12 characters — upper, lower, numbers, symbols
Use a unique password for every platform — never reuse
Use Think B2B's approved password manager (ask Singularitee)
Change passwords immediately if compromise suspected
Use a passphrase: Coffee!Client$Brief2026
❌ NEVER DO THIS
Use your name, pet, birthday or "Think B2B"
Share your password with a colleague — ever
Reuse the same password for Salesforce, M365 and email
Write passwords on sticky notes near your screen
💡Think B2B context: Salesforce CRM, Mailchimp and client SharePoint portals are high-value targets. Each needs its own strong, unique password.
Section 2 — Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA — Your Second Line of Defence

MFA blocks 99.9% of automated credential attacks (Microsoft Security). Even with your password stolen, attackers cannot get in without your second factor.

ENABLE MFA ON ALL OF THESE
📧Microsoft 365 / Teams / SharePoint
📊Salesforce CRM
📬Mailchimp & email marketing
💰Any financial platform or client portal
⚠️ CRITICAL WARNING
Unexpected MFA prompt?
If you receive an MFA notification when you are NOT logging in — someone already has your password. Deny immediately. Report to Emma Czirok.
A team member received an M365 MFA prompt at 11pm while watching TV. They denied it and called IT — a password had been exposed in a public breach. One correct action prevented a full compromise.
Section 3 — Phishing, Vishing & Smishing

Recognising the Attack

Phishing causes 84% of all cyber incidents. As an agency communicating with dozens of clients and vendors daily, we are a prime target.

THREE ATTACK TYPES
📧 Phishing (Email)
Fraudulent emails disguised as Microsoft, a client or supplier — prompting you to log in or open an attachment
📞 Vishing (Phone)
Calls pretending to be IT support, HMRC or a colleague — requesting access, passwords or urgent payments
💬 Smishing (SMS/WhatsApp)
Text messages from fake couriers, banks or the company asking you to click a link
RED FLAGS TO SPOT
Urgency: "Act now or your account will be closed"
Misspelled domain: support@micros0ft.com
Unexpected attachment from a known contact
Request for password, MFA code, or bank details
Client asking for invoice payment to a new bank account

Section 4 — Sophisticated Scams

AI, Deepfakes & Social Engineering

Attackers now clone voices, replicate email styles, and build pixel-perfect fake login pages using AI. The verification habit is your only reliable defence.

MODERN ATTACK TECHNIQUES
CEO Fraud: Email appearing to be from Emma Czirok requesting urgent payment to a new supplier
AI Voice Clone: Call sounding exactly like a colleague asking for login help
Fake SharePoint page: Identical clone of our login screen harvesting your credentials
THE PAUSE-AND-VERIFY RULE

Before acting on any urgent request:

❓ Was I expecting this?
❓ Is urgency designed to stop me thinking?
❓ Can I verify via a completely different channel?
❓ Would they normally ask this way?
If ANY answer is uncertain — STOP and verify first.
Section 5 — Remote Working

Staying Secure Outside the Office

Same standards at home as in the office — no exceptions.

💻 DEVICE
Lock screen when stepping away
Auto screen lock: max 5 minutes
Keep OS & software updated
Never let others use your work device
📡 NETWORK
Never use public Wi-Fi for work
Use mobile hotspot if unsure
Home Wi-Fi: WPA2 or WPA3 only
🏠 DATA AT HOME
Never save to personal iCloud / Google Drive
All files → SharePoint only
Ensure screen not visible to others
💡 Scenario: You're presenting a Salesforce pipeline on a Teams call from home. Your partner walks behind you. That screen has client deal values and contact names. Position your screen so only you can see it.

Section 7 — Legal & Compliance

UK GDPR, ISO 27001 & EcoVadis

Cyber security is a legal obligation. Breaches can mean regulatory fines, client contract penalties and reputational damage that directly impacts our ability to win business.

⚖️
UK GDPR / DPA 2018
ICO fines up to £17.5M or 4% global turnover. Requires appropriate technical measures to protect personal data.
🔐
ISO/IEC 27001
Think B2B aligns with this standard. Annex A.7.2.2 requires staff security training — this module is that control.
🌿
EcoVadis Rating
Assessed under Business Ethics pillar. Poor scores affect procurement-driven contract wins.
COMPUTER MISUSE ACT 1990
Unauthorised access to any computer system is a criminal offence — including accessing a colleague's account even with good intentions.
72-HOUR ICO NOTIFICATION
Think B2B must notify the ICO within 72 hours of a serious personal data breach. Early internal reporting is critical.
Section 8 — Incident Response

What To Do If Something Goes Wrong

Act quickly, report honestly. There is no blame for reporting. There IS liability for concealing.

5-STEP RESPONSE PROTOCOL
STOP — Close browser/email. Disconnect Wi-Fi if actively under attack.
REPORT — Emma Czirok: emma.czirok@thinkb2bmarketing.com / 01977 708643
NOTIFY — Cath Mace + support@singularitee.co.uk
DOCUMENT — What happened, when, what data may be affected
AWAIT GUIDANCE — Do not fix or delete anything without IT direction
NEVER DO THE FOLLOWING
Try to resolve it without IT involvement
Delete files or emails related to the incident
Delay reporting hoping it "resolves itself"
Discuss externally or on social media
⏱️ ICO obligation: report serious breaches within 72 hours. Early internal reporting gives us time to act.
